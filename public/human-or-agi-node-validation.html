What needs validation?

Use plain language. Do not paste private, customer, secret, wallet, payment, credential, or regulated data.

How to choose validation authority
AGI Node

Best for public-safe deterministic checks: schema, route fit, claim boundary, replay readiness, Evidence Docket completeness.

Human

Best for public claims, legal/financial/security posture, final publication, and any judgment-heavy decision.

Hybrid

Best default for important work: AGI Node precheck first, human final review second.

Council

Best for Loop→RSI, Move‑37, strategic, high-novelty, or institutional promotion decisions.

Authority matrix

State-of-the-art validation is not “more autonomy everywhere.” It is the right authority for the right proof path.

AGI Node validator

  • Schema and route fit
  • Claim-boundary wording
  • Evidence Docket completeness
  • Replay-readiness
  • Token / data boundary consistency

Human reviewer

  • Final public claims
  • Legal / financial / security posture
  • Publication approval
  • High-impact decisions
  • External-action authorization

Hybrid

  • AGI Node precheck
  • Human final review
  • Important public proof pages
  • Procurement and pilot decisions
  • Validator-ready claims

Architect / Validator Council

  • Loop → RSI governance
  • Move‑37 candidates
  • Strategic dossiers
  • High-novelty claims
  • Institutional promotion
