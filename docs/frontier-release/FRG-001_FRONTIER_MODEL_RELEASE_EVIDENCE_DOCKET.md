
# FRG‑001 — Frontier Model Release Evidence Docket Standard

## Purpose

FRG‑001 defines a practical Evidence Docket for high-consequence frontier AI release decisions.

It is designed for situations where a model, capability, access tier, preview program, cyber-relevant capability, restricted deployment, suspension, or restore decision requires evidence rather than informal authority.

## Core principle

```text
Do not argue from authority. Argue from the docket.
```

## Required objects

1. **Mission Contract** — the precise release decision under review.
2. **Claims Matrix** — bounded claims, evidence, counterevidence, and uncertainty.
3. **Evidence Docket** — public evidence, private annex index, tool traces, and source provenance.
4. **Access Tier Matrix** — public, limited preview, trusted access, restricted, denied, suspended, restored.
5. **Validator Reports** — cybersecurity, model behavior, policy/legal, public-interest, enterprise risk.
6. **Risk Ledger** — misuse, geopolitical, operational, economic, customer, and public-trust risks.
7. **Rollback / Restore Protocol** — conditions for suspension, remediation, and reopening.
8. **Governed Decision State** — explicit decision state and authority boundary.
9. **Chronicle** — append-only record of what was known, changed, reviewed, and decided.

## Non-goals

FRG‑001 does not certify safety, replace legal review, replace government authority, expose sensitive eval details, or guarantee correct decisions.

## Publication model

- **Public layer:** redacted source ledger, claims summary, decision state, validator summary, limitations.
- **Private annex:** sensitive evals, exploit details, customer records, legal analysis, classified or controlled material.
- **Human authority:** final authorization remains outside the console.
