# Board and Investor Evidence Room

## Purpose

The evidence room is a structured index for serious stakeholders. It is not marketing copy. It organizes proof artifacts so reviewers can assess capability, demand, governance, security, and commercial progress.

## Sections

1. Executive summary.
2. Product architecture.
3. Proof system.
4. Evidence Dockets.
5. Pilot records.
6. Usage metrics.
7. Benchmark results.
8. Commercial records.
9. Security and risk posture.
10. Governance and claim boundary.
11. Independent validation.
12. Open questions.

## Review principle

A stakeholder should be able to distinguish what is built, what is tested, what is validated, what is confidential, and what remains unproven.


## Claim discipline

This document is an operating standard. It does not claim financing, market valuation, production authorization, legal approval, user-fund authorization, revenue, enterprise adoption, or independent audit completion. Counted evidence must be dated, source-verifiable, and stored in the evidence room.
