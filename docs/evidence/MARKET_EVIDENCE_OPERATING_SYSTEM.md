# Market Evidence Operating System

## Objective

This operating system converts market interest into proof-bearing artifacts. It prevents vague traction claims and turns each signal into a structured record.

## Inputs

- Customer discovery notes.
- Design partner commitments.
- Pilot outcome dockets.
- Usage metrics.
- Benchmark reports.
- Commercial records.
- Partner records.
- Independent validator reports.
- Fair-value-support documents.

## Workflow

1. Capture the raw signal.
2. Classify it by evidence category.
3. Store the artifact in `evidence/`.
4. Mark whether it is a placeholder, operator-attested, source-verifiable, or third-party-verified.
5. Run `python scripts/validate_market_evidence.py`.
6. Review the generated reports.
7. Publish only the claims that the gate allows.

## Why this matters

A repository can look impressive without proving demand. The evidence operating system makes demand, usage, outcomes, validation, and commercial pull visible without exaggeration.


## Claim discipline

This document is an operating standard. It does not claim financing, market valuation, production authorization, legal approval, user-fund authorization, revenue, enterprise adoption, or independent audit completion. Counted evidence must be dated, source-verifiable, and stored in the evidence room.
