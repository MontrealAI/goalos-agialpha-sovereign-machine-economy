# Product Principles

## 1. Proof before prestige

The project may be grand in ambition, but its public claims must be smaller than its evidence.

## 2. Non-technical first

A founder, operator, researcher, or executive should understand what the repository does before reading code.

## 3. Machine-readable by default

Every important artifact should have a schema: mission, job, trace, proof bundle, docket, validator report, risk ledger, settlement receipt, Chronicle entry, capability package.

## 4. Humans keep authority

The workflow automates generation and QA, but the claim boundary and release posture make clear that human review remains required for consequential deployment.

## 5. Reuse is the compounding engine

The most valuable output is not one answer. It is a reusable capability package with proof, limitations, and replay instructions.
